What follows is my attempt to transcribe and annotate the various rules for Blackstone Fortress which we/I hope to use and enjoy.

So far, this includes rules from;

  • Warhammer Quest: Blackstone Fortress (the core set)
  • August 2019 White Dwarf Precipice

… and some corrections from the FaceBook group who tend to be lovely and non-spammy.

Setup

“Setup” isn’t too hard … once you’ve done it. You get out all the parts you’ll use for your expedition and plop them on the table.

I’ve generally found that 24x36 inch table is a bit cramped, but, safe for all the bits, dice, and inevitable snacks. I also like using a solid colour blanket as a tablecloth to make the board easier to look at.

Explorers

Players select four1 explorer cards and take their minis out, and the ships cards for these explorers. If there are less than four spaceships - players pick whatever ships they want until they have four. Finally, the explorers can trade any cards the heroes are carrying2 before placing their miniatures on one of the MagLev tiles and somewhere on the table.3

Decks

Decks are (ostensibly) setup slightly differently depending upon the Expedition’s goals. All variants require the Discovery deck of archotech and clues, the Encounter deck of monsters with their Hostile Reference cards, and any “Fortress Upgrade” legacy cards that have been activated.

Expedition (for Clue cards)

This is the “generic” game where the Explorers are trying to find clues that will lead them to a “Hidden Vault” within the fortress. Shuffle each of the two big “Exploration” decks and draw four cards from each, then shuffle those eight cards together. This creates a deck with four “Combat” cards and four “Challenge” cards which will be a surprise to all players.

Recovery Stage

After resolving an Exploration card, follow these steps;

  1. roll a “Recovery Check” for any explorer who is out of action
    • roll the d20
    • if it’s less than the number of “Grievous Wounds” on the Explorer’s card; the explorer is “dead”
      • give the explorer a “Mortis” card - they are dead and cannot trade anything under any circumstances
      • the explorer may not trade or act anymore9
  2. if any explorers (now) have a Mortis card; the expedition returns to Precipice
  3. if the players all agree; the expedition returns to Precipice
  4. do up to one Recuperate action per Explorer
    • it feels odd that the Explorers can’t recover ALL non-Grievous Wounds … so … let’s say that they’re rushing to get in and out before the Fortress can get them

Facillities Step

Following the Legacy Step any (surviginv) Explorer who participated in the expedition may perform the Facillities Step to advance themselves witht he resources of Precipice. Each Explorer’s ship offers a facility and a selection of Resource Cards that can be purchased with ArchaeoTech claimed from the Fortress. Traders can’t “make change” but the explorer’s each get 1 free point on their home ship. Before trading on a ship - the explorer may choose to utilise the ship’s Facillities, possibly gaining or benefitting from their existing Resource Cards. Certain explorers have (what I call) a “home turf re-roll” for the Facillities step when they’re on their own ship. Finally, if none of the spacecraft interest an Explorer, they may visiti Precipice itself The facilities all have “home turf” advantages to allow an explorer re-roll if it’s their own ship.

Precipice

Instead of visiting a spacecraft and making use of its facilities, an explorer can visit Precipice itself. To do this, that explorer must pick one of the four following locations within Precipice to visit and roll the Blackstone dice on the corresponding chart to see what befalls their explorer during their stay. In all cases, any wound counters applied to explorers as a result of visiting Precipice itself will remain on their explorer cards for their next expedition. If an explorer is taken out of action, do not make a recovery roll for them (they do not die) but keep their wound counters as described above. If an explorer is placed into a stasis vault after visiting Precipice, place their wound counters (with the current side facing up) into the stasis vault with them. It is also possible for an explorer to gain inspiration points whilst visiting Precipice. An explorer keeps any inspiration points gained this way for their next expedition. If they are placed into a stasis chamber, place their inspiration point counters into the stasis chamber with them with the relevant side face-up.

Expedition 1 / An Inauspicious Start

Precipice

Janus Draik, Dahyak Grekh, Amallyn Shadowguide and the Ratling Twins, Rein and Raus came together and entered one of the functioning MagLev chambers headed towards the locations Amallyn and Janus had earmarked. The initial exchange of pleasantries escalated and once the chamber deposited the explorers the Twins and Amallyn began exchanging blows over the term “monkeih.” The Twins collected a sprain dampening their willingness to fight while Janus baited Amallyn to cease with the discovery of a Wraithbone artefact containing Remnants of the Fall.

As another transport deposited the group in another chamber, they spotted shadowy figures and Dahyak dashed forward and planted one of his hunting traps. Two groups of guardsmen were assisting a group of cultists whose corrupted mecha dendrites crawled over an improvised dais. Amallyn and Raus took aim at the approaching hostiles and made ready to open fire as Janus and Dahyakprepared to dispatch these ragtag rebels. After the initial exchange of gunfire, several of the guardsmen lay on the floor gasping while Janus squared off against the four cultists and Rein used his grapple to reach the dais and pocketed The Empryian Chronometer. A stray weapon shot activated some fortress system, and the numerous piles of debris all had been hiding from became unstable enough that navigating the room would be challenging and slow. The fight quickly threatened to overwhelm the explorers; a guardsman charged Rein while detonating a grenade while his comrades opened fire scoring hits on the ratling. With his brother out of action - Raus became hysteric and unable to focus on anything other than resuing his brother. While Janus and Amallyn were able to subdue the Negavolt Cultists the Guardsmen swarmed Dahyak almost as if they were familiar with the tracker’s prowess. Screaming from the far end of the room - one of the Negavolt Cultists arose in the far corner of the room with unholy vigour ready to threaten the explorers again.

Determined to retain The Empryian Chronometer Janus assisted Raus in dragging Rein to the nearest exit chamber summoning a MagLev to take them away from this chaos. Injured and bleeding Dahyak thought of the facilities that awaited him and was able to viperously dispatch the cultists threatening him. As Dahyak slid into the chamber the blast from another traitor’s grenade threw him against the wall while leaving Janus unscathed. Disgusted with the animal’s ineptness and frustrated by the monkeih’s inability to overcome their own degenerate fallen kinsman Amallyn shouldered her rifle, and traversed the entirety of the room in a blink and sent the MagLev transport to exit the area.

Precipice

The injuries sustained by Dahyak and The Twins were not mortal, however, the group decided to return to Precipice regardless. The Twins begrudgingly surrendered The Empryian Chronometer to Janus with the understanding that the group would await their recovery before returning tot he fortress. Neither the deserters nor the Rouge Traitor expected the oath to be fulfilled, and, when none were looking The Twins combined a spare component discovered with previous salvage in their cargo bay to produce a valuable but useless archotech trinket. Having explored the fortress for somewhat longer than he’d previously admitted, Dahyak warned the group that the perception of an Astra Militarum and Aeldari alliance operating with a Rouge trader would garner the attention of more powerful foes from within the fortress. as Dahyak described the foes to Janus, Amallyn recognised the monsters of whom he spoke; traitor legionaries, veterans of the long war and foes she hadn’t expected would feel the need to attend to this place.

Clearly, the fortress held secrets beyond those that Janus and Amallyn had sought for their own interests and threats beyond those that had just defeated them.
